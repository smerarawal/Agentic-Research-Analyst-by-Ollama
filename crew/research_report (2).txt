Executive Summary:
The NVIDIA Blackwell GPU architecture consumes up to 1,400W per chip. A fully loaded GB300 NVL72 rack, containing 72 of these GPUs, pulls 132 to 140 kW under normal operation. This is significantly higher than the global average rack density in data centers, which sits at roughly 8 kW. According to Uptime Institute's 2024 survey, only about 1% of data center operators currently meet the power requirements needed for a fully loaded B300. The UK Power Networks (UKPN) dataset starts at the beginning of 2023 and runs to April 2026, providing context for NVIDIA's Blackwell GPU deployment in data centers.
