**Executive Summary**

NVIDIA's AI strategy in data center chips has been a driving force behind the company's growth and dominance in the industry. This report provides an in-depth analysis of NVIDIA's core strategy, technical capabilities, competitive landscape, risks and challenges, and future outlook.

**Core Strategy**

NVIDIA's core strategy is centered around its transition from being a component supplier to becoming the primary architect of the global AI power infrastructure. This shift has enabled the company to develop system-level power solutions, such as its 800 VDC power architecture, and bundle six co-designed chips into a single architecture with the Vera Rubin platform.

**Technical Analysis**

NVIDIA's technical capabilities are showcased through its development of innovative hardware and software solutions for the AI data center market. The company's NVLink Fusion interconnect strategy enables hyperscalers to integrate their own custom ASICs, further solidifying its position in the industry.

**Competitive Landscape**

The competitive landscape is marked by NVIDIA's dominant position in the AI chip market, with an estimated over 80% market share. However, this dominance comes with significant dependencies and risks due to global energy constraints.

**Risks and Challenges**

Despite its strengths, NVIDIA faces several risks and challenges, including:

1. The company's reliance on external partnerships and collaborations to control and standardize the entire AI data center value chain.
2. The potential for increased competition in the AI chip market as new entrants emerge.
3. The need to ensure reliable energy supplies for its data centers, which may be affected by global energy constraints.

**Future Outlook**

NVIDIA's future outlook is marked by its goal to build itself into a one-stop service platform, offering all key data center elements beyond high-end chip hardware. This vision is driven by CEO Jensen Huang's desire to transform NVIDIA into a comprehensive service provider, enabling the company to expand its influence in the AI and data center markets.

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, NVIDIA's AI strategy in data center chips has been a driving force behind the company's growth and dominance in the industry. While the company faces risks and challenges, its technical capabilities, competitive landscape, and future outlook all point towards continued success and leadership in the market.
